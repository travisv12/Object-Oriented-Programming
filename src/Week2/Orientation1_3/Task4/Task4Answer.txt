Understanding various probability distributions is essential for accurately modeling real-world phenomena in simulations. Here are some commonly used distributions and their typical applications:

1. Normal Distribution:

- Description: Also known as the Gaussian distribution, it is characterized by its symmetric, bell-shaped curve centered around the mean.
- Applications:
  - Height and Weight: In large populations, human heights and weights tend to follow a normal distribution.
  - Test Scores: Standardized test results often exhibit a normal distribution pattern.
  - Measurement Errors: Random errors in measurements frequently conform to a normal distribution.
- Reference: https://www.statology.org/example-of-normal-distribution/

2. Exponential Distribution:

- Description: This distribution models the time between events in a Poisson process, where events occur continuously and independently at a constant average rate.
- Applications:
  - Time Between Failures: Used to model the time until a system or component fails.
  - Service Times: Describes the time between arrivals of customers in a queue.
  - Radioactive Decay: Models the time until a radioactive particle decays.
- Reference: https://www.statology.org/exponential-distribution-real-life-examples/

3. Poisson Distribution:

- Description: Models the number of events occurring within a fixed interval of time or space, given the events happen at a constant rate and independently of the time since the last event.
- Applications:
  - Call Centers: Predicting the number of incoming calls per hour.
  - Traffic Flow: Estimating the number of cars passing through a checkpoint in a given time frame.
  - Biology: Counting the number of mutations in a strand of DNA over a certain length.

4. Uniform Distribution:

- Description: All outcomes are equally likely within a defined range.
- Applications:
  - Random Number Generation: Generating numbers where each has an equal chance of being selected.
  - Simulations: Modeling scenarios where each outcome within a range is equally probable.

5. Binomial Distribution:

- Description: Models the number of successes in a fixed number of independent Bernoulli trials, each with the same probability of success.
- Applications:
  - Quality Control: Determining the number of defective items in a batch.
  - Survey Analysis: Estimating the number of people who will respond positively to a survey question.

Selecting the appropriate distribution depends on the specific characteristics of the phenomenon being modeled, such as the nature of the events, their independence, and the intervals at which they occur.
